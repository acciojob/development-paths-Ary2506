Here are five common career paths for software developers: Front-End Developer Specializes in creating the user interface (UI) and user experience (UX) for websites or applications. They work with technologies like HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and frameworks such as React, Angular, or Vue.js. Back-End Developer Focuses on the server-side of applications, handling databases, APIs, and the logic that powers the front-end. They typically work with server-side languages like Python, Java, Ruby, PHP, Node.js, and databases such as MySQL, PostgreSQL, or MongoDB. Full-Stack Developer A combination of both front-end and back-end development. Full-stack developers are proficient in both client-side and server-side technologies, and they have the ability to build entire applications from start to finish. DevOps Engineer Works on automating and streamlining the development, deployment, and operational processes. They focus on integrating development with operations, utilizing tools like Docker, Kubernetes, Jenkins, and cloud platforms such as AWS, Azure, or Google Cloud. Software Architect A more senior position, responsible for designing the overall structure and high-level design of software systems. They make key decisions about technologies, system design, and frameworks to ensure scalability, performance, and maintainability.
